16 | This simple application demonstrates the use of the components of |
17 | an edge detector: Gaussian, gradient, gradient of Gaussian, Laplacian, |
18 | Laplacian of Gaussian, zero crossings, nonmaximum suppression, |
19 | hysteresis thresholding. In other words, the components that make up |
20 | the Marr-Hildreth and Canny (and other) edge detectors. Complete edge |
21 | detector classes are (going to be) implemented in the library, so this |
22 | one is really for people who want to roll their own operator. |
23 | |
24 | The original image is displayed in the upper-left subwindow. |
25 | The upper-right quadrant shows the image to which has been applied a |
26 | Gaussian filter os cale selectable through a right-click menu |
27 | The lower-left quadrant shows the result of applying to the U-R image |
28 | a Laplacian Sobel operator or LoG + zero-crossing detector |
29 | The lower-right quadrant show the effect of applying to the U-L image |
30 | a gradient of Gaussian operator or GoG + nonmaximum supressor + |
